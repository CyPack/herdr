# Module Governance 2.1.0

The Ratatui intelligence module is a fail-closed research, comparison, and
adaptation boundary for agents and humans.

## Authority

It may freeze reference inputs, use Codebase Memory, write corpus and analysis
artifacts, classify cross-stack behavior, and produce testable integration
plans. It may not change Herdr product code, contact or restart the stable
runtime, adopt dependencies, merge, fast-forward, or push merely because P14
passes.

## Evidence and status

Every graph-dependent claim requires real MCP evidence. Missing MCP capability
makes the relevant work `blocked`; incomplete but usable evidence makes it
`partial`. Neither status may be silently promoted. Evidence locations,
confidence, semantic differences, failure differences, performance differences,
ownership differences, license constraints, acceptance tests, and cross tests
must remain traceable.

## Development discipline

Contract changes follow TDD and RED-GREEN-REFACTOR. Use targeted staging,
atomic lowercase conventional commits, and fresh validator evidence. Preserve
the P0-P14 compatibility family and reject unknown or lossy translations.

## Delivery boundary

P14 emits a reference-adapter input for the sibling `herdr-change-pipeline`.
That handoff does not grant product authority. CyPack publication and shared
checkout fast-forward require their own approved gate and fresh verification.
